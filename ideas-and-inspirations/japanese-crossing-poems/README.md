# Japanese Crossing Poems

These three poems are a small source set for Musia songs about moonlight,
sea-crossing, exile, home, and the Tang-era China-Japan cultural passage.

## 1. 静夜思

```text
《静夜思》
唐 · 李白

床前明月光，
疑是地上霜。
举头望明月，
低头思故乡。
```

Song direction: intimate moonlit homesickness. Best as a sparse lullaby or
quiet chamber-pop song.

## 2. 哭晁卿衡

```text
《哭晁卿衡》
唐 · 李白

日本晁卿辞帝都，
征帆一片绕蓬壶。
明月不归沉碧海，
白云愁色满苍梧。
```

Song direction: a Tang elegy for Abe no Nakamaro / Chao Qingheng, imagined as
a ship, moon, blue sea, and white clouds carrying grief. The Chinese lyric
constraint for the current Musia song is strict: every Chinese sung line should
come from this original poem only. English and Japanese can act as companion
lines around the poem.

Important pronunciation notes:

```text
晁卿衡: cháo qīng héng
帝都: dì dū
征帆: zhēng fān
蓬壶: péng hú
沉碧海: chén bì hǎi
苍梧: cāng wú
```

## 3. 送僧归日本

```text
《送僧归日本》
唐 · 钱起

上国随缘住，
来途若梦行。
浮天沧海远，
去世法舟轻。
水月通禅寂，
鱼龙听梵声。
惟怜一灯影，
万里眼中明。
```

Song direction: meditative sea-crossing, Buddhist light, water-moon imagery,
and one lamp shining across ten thousand li. Note that this poem is by 钱起,
not 李白.

## Current Song Plan

Current target: `哭晁卿衡 · Moon Over The Blue Sea`.

The first generation should be a mixed-language ACE-Step song:

- active vocal: Mandarin Chinese original poem lines + English lines + Japanese
  romaji lines;
- Chinese public track: original poem lines only, repeated as source anchors;
- English and Japanese public tracks: companion translations/adaptations;
- style: cinematic ocean elegy, clear female vocal, slow art-pop ballad, pipa
  or guqin color, piano, strings, soft low percussion, spacious sea reverb;
- quality goal: beautiful melody and audible vocal first; exact line recovery
  second, corrected after ASR/listening.
